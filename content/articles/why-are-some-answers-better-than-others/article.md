## Traceability builds trust

When an answer depends on facts, the reader should be able to tell where those facts came from. That does not mean dumping a bibliography onto every ordinary sentence. It means linking the claims that matter and preferring sources close to the evidence: official guidance, standards, textbooks, universities, peer-reviewed research, and direct documentation.

Purdue Online Writing Lab’s guidance on paragraph development emphasizes specific evidence plus analysis—not evidence dropped on the page and left to fend for itself. A source should support the reasoning; it should not replace it.

## A useful answer is a small teaching system

The best practical answers do more than close one question. They leave the reader better equipped for the next version of the problem. That is why How Biscuit pages use worked examples, common mistakes, source notes, evidence labels, cheap safe alternatives, and related guides.
