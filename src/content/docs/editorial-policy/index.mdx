---
title: Editorial Policy
description: The publishing standards How Biscuit uses for clarity, evidence, safety, and updates.
kind: trust
updatedDate: 2026-07-18
---

How Biscuit articles should answer the question directly before adding context. Readers should not have to scan past a long introduction to find the useful part.

Factual claims should be traceable to a source, an observable test, or a clear reasoning trail. Safety, money, health, legal, repair, and product claims need extra caution and should use risk badges or explicit caveats when published.

Articles can be updated when the explanation becomes clearer, when source evidence changes, or when a correction is needed.

Evidence labels use a bounded vocabulary: Hands-on tested, Owner experience, Specification reviewed, Researched, Price listing only, and Editorial standard. Specification review is never described as testing.

AI may help with research planning, outlines, drafts, examples, and QA checks. AI is not treated as a final authority, and it must not be used to invent sources, testing, credentials, reviews, authors, or product experience.
