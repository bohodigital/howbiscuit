## Find the wattage

1. Check the rating label near the power cord, back panel, underside, battery compartment, or power adapter.
2. Look for **W** or **watts**. If the label only lists volts and amps, watts are approximately volts × amps.
3. For a computer, refrigerator, air conditioner, or anything that cycles, the label is usually a maximum—not the average. A plug-in power meter gives a better result.
4. Use the electricity rate on your bill when possible. Divide the electricity charge by billed kWh if the bill does not print the rate directly.
