## Calculators

Use a calculator when a formula produces a meaningful result. Show the formula, units, rounding, valid input range, and assumptions beside the answer.

## Checklists

Use checklists for setup, diagnosis, inspection, maintenance, comparison, and handoff tasks where missing a step matters more than computing a number.

## Converters

Cover measurements, temperature, cooking quantities, storage, networking, and other conversions with clear unit labels and appropriate precision.

## Cost Estimators

Separate upfront costs, recurring costs, energy, consumables, maintenance, repair, replacement, and likely service life.

## Templates

Provide reusable comparison sheets, inventories, maintenance logs, troubleshooting records, and project-planning formats.

## Decision Tools

Use auditable comparisons and decision trees. Do not hide subjective priorities behind a single score or imply precision the inputs cannot support.
