Buying Guides explains what features matter for a defined job, how much ownership actually costs, and when keeping, repairing, borrowing, or buying used is the better choice.

## Browse Buying Guides

<TopicDirectory topics={buyingGuideTopics} />

<h2 id="computers-laptops">Computers & Laptops</h2>

Begin with workload, software, screen, ports, battery, upgradeability, repairability, support life, and total cost. Brand is not a substitute for requirements.

## Networking

Match routers, mesh systems, modems, switches, and access points to the home layout, internet plan, wired connections, device count, and management needs.

## Smart Home

Compare compatibility, required hubs, local control, cloud dependence, subscriptions, privacy, support history, and what happens if the vendor ends the service.

## Kitchen

Evaluate capacity, power, controls, cleaning, storage, replacement parts, repairability, and whether a simpler tool performs the same job.

<h2 id="home-diy">Home & DIY</h2>

Compare tools, materials, cleaning equipment, storage, consumables, warranties, rentals, and expected frequency of use.

<h2 id="used-refurbished">Used & Refurbished</h2>

Check condition, battery or wear items, remaining support, locks, included accessories, return rights, warranty, repair cost, and the price difference from new.

No guide should claim hands-on testing unless How Biscuit actually tested the product or method. Research, specification comparison, and direct testing must remain clearly labeled.
