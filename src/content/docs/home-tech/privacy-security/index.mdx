Privacy & Security covers the routine steps that reduce the chance of account loss, data loss, unwanted tracking, and insecure devices at home. Guides explain the tradeoffs instead of presenting every optional setting as an emergency.

## Start With the High-Value Basics

1. Install supported operating-system, browser, router, and device updates.
2. Use unique passwords and a reputable password manager.
3. Enable multifactor authentication, beginning with email and financial accounts.
4. Maintain a backup that can be restored without the original device.
5. Protect the router administration account and remove unknown devices.
6. Review app permissions and connected accounts periodically.

These steps usually matter more than obscure “hardening” tweaks that make a device difficult to use or recover.

## What Belongs Here

- account protection and recovery
- backups and restore testing
- safer Windows, macOS, browser, phone, and router defaults
- home-network and smart-device security
- app permissions, telemetry, advertising identifiers, and tracking controls
- phishing, malicious downloads, scams, and suspicious account activity

## Stop and Get Help When

An account has financial access, identity documents are exposed, a device may contain evidence needed by law enforcement or an employer, or recovery steps could destroy important data. Preserve the information you have and use the appropriate provider, financial institution, workplace, or qualified professional.
